### Step 2: Define Your Automating Business Workflows Objectives Clearly

Before asking Salesforce Einstein to do anything, write down exactly what you want to accomplish. For automating business workflows, this means specifying:

- **The deliverable**: What is the final output you need?
- **Quality standards**: What does 'good enough' look like?
- **Constraints**: Are there style guidelines, length requirements, or technical specifications?
- **Timeline**: How quickly do you need results?

This preparation step saves enormous time. Users who skip it typically spend twice as long iterating on outputs because they are refining their requirements and their approach simultaneously.

### Step 3: Leverage Predictive lead scoring for Initial Output

With your objectives defined, it is time to engage Salesforce Einstein's primary capability: **Predictive lead scoring**.

Start with a clear, specific input. Vague requests produce vague results. Instead of asking for generic output, provide context about your requirements, audience, and desired format.

Key principle: your first output from Salesforce Einstein is rarely your final output. Think of it as a high-quality first draft that you will refine. This mindset shift -- from expecting perfection to expecting a strong starting point -- is crucial for productive use.

### Step 4: Iterate and Refine Your Results

The iteration phase is where Salesforce Einstein truly shines for automating business workflows. Take your initial output and evaluate it against your objectives from Step 2.

Common refinement strategies:

- **Specificity**: If the output is too generic, add more constraints and context to your follow-up requests.
- **Decomposition**: Break complex tasks into smaller, more manageable pieces. Salesforce Einstein handles focused requests better than broad ones.
- **Reference-based refinement**: Share examples of your desired output style and ask Salesforce Einstein to adjust accordingly.

Most professional users go through 2-4 iteration cycles before reaching a satisfactory result. This is normal and expected -- not a sign that the tool is underperforming.

Before considering your automating business workflows work complete, run through this quality checklist:

## Mistakes That Will Cost You Time

Learn from our errors:

### 1. Using Salesforce Einstein Without Clear Objectives

The most common mistake we see is launching Salesforce Einstein and improvising. Without defined goals, you end up iterating aimlessly and wasting time. Always complete the objective-setting step before generating any output.

### 2. Accepting First-Pass Output Without Review

AI-generated content from Salesforce Einstein can look polished on the surface while containing subtle errors, inconsistencies, or hallucinations. Always review and verify output before using it in any professional context.

### 3. Over-Prompting or Under-Prompting

There is a sweet spot for input detail. Too little context produces generic results. Too much context can confuse the model or cause it to fixate on irrelevant details. Aim for clear, specific, and concise inputs.

### 4. Ignoring Salesforce Einstein's Advanced Features

Many users stick to the basic functionality and miss the advanced features that deliver the most value for automating business workflows. Invest time in learning the full feature set -- the ROI is substantial.

### 5. Not Iterating Enough

Professional results typically require 2-4 refinement cycles. Users who expect perfection on the first try often get frustrated and abandon Salesforce Einstein prematurely. Treat the first output as a draft, not a deliverable.
