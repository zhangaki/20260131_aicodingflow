## Common Mistakes to Avoid

We made these mistakes so you do not have to. Here are the most frequent pitfalls when using Windsurf for rapid prototyping with ai assistance:

### 1. Using Windsurf Without Clear Objectives

The most common mistake we see is launching Windsurf and improvising. Without defined goals, you end up iterating aimlessly and wasting time. Always complete the objective-setting step before generating any output.

### 2. Accepting First-Pass Output Without Review

AI-generated content from Windsurf can look polished on the surface while containing subtle errors, inconsistencies, or hallucinations. Always review and verify output before using it in any professional context.

### 3. Over-Prompting or Under-Prompting

There is a sweet spot for input detail. Too little context produces generic results. Too much context can confuse the model or cause it to fixate on irrelevant details. Aim for clear, specific, and concise inputs.

### 4. Ignoring Windsurf's Advanced Features

Many users stick to the basic functionality and miss the advanced features that deliver the most value for rapid prototyping with ai assistance. Invest time in learning the full feature set -- the ROI is substantial.

### 5. Not Iterating Enough

Professional results typically require 2-4 refinement cycles. Users who expect perfection on the first try often get frustrated and abandon Windsurf prematurely. Treat the first output as a draft, not a deliverable.
