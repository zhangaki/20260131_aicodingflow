You’re losing hours to two things: recurring meetings and repeatable busywork (writing, scheduling, follow-ups).

Outcome: by the end of this list, you’ll have 2–3 AI tools you can set up this week to cut meeting time, automate routine tasks, and protect focus blocks.

Next step: skim the 10 categories, pick one tool per pain point, then run the checklist under each section (10 minutes) to confirm it’s worth keeping.

## 1. AI-Powered Writing Assistants

### Tool:
- Grammarly (consider: ProWritingAid)

### Automation:
- Grammar and spelling checks, style suggestions, tone rewrites.

### Actionable Checklist:
- **Error Catch Rate:** Paste a paragraph containing 10 intentional errors (typos + grammar). Does it flag and fix at least 8? (Pass/Fail: `>=8/10`).
- **Tone Rewrite:** Paste: “I am very disappointed with the service.” Can it rewrite into (1) neutral and (2) firm-but-professional versions? (Pass/Fail: 2 usable rewrites).

## 2. Meeting Transcription + Summaries

### Tool:
- Otter.ai (consider: Fireflies.ai)

### Automation:
- Auto-transcribe calls, summarize, extract action items, searchable meeting archive.

### Actionable Checklist:
- **Action Items:** Run it on a 15–30 minute meeting. Does the summary include at least 3 concrete action items with owners/dates you can verify? (Pass/Fail: `>=3`).
- **Searchability:** Search for a unique phrase someone said. Can you jump to the correct timestamp within 10 seconds? (Pass/Fail).

## 3. Email Drafting + Inbox Triage

### Tool:
- Gmail/Google Workspace with Gemini (consider: Microsoft Copilot in Outlook)

### Automation:
- Draft replies, summarize long threads, suggest follow-ups.

### Actionable Checklist:
- **Thread Summary:** Pick a long email thread (10+ messages). Does it summarize the decision + open questions accurately in 5 bullets or less? (Pass/Fail).
- **Draft Quality:** Generate 3 replies (yes/no/needs-more-info). Would you send at least 2 with light edits (not a full rewrite)? (Pass/Fail: `>=2/3`).

## 4. Calendar Scheduling + Availability

### Tool:
- Calendly (consider: Google Appointment Schedule)

### Automation:
- Share booking links, enforce buffer times, auto-timezone handling.

### Actionable Checklist:
- **Focus Protection:** Add (1) 15-min buffers, (2) max 3 meetings/day, (3) no-meeting mornings. Does the booking page enforce all three? (Pass/Fail).
- **Timezone Test:** Book from a different timezone (or use an incognito browser + simulated TZ). Does the invite land at the correct local time? (Pass/Fail).

## 5. No-Code Automations (If-This-Then-That)

### Tool:
- Zapier (consider: Make)

### Automation:
- Trigger-based workflows across apps (Slack, Gmail, Sheets, Notion, etc.).

### Actionable Checklist:
- **One Real Zap:** Build: “New Gmail label = `Invoice` → save attachment to Drive → post Slack message.” Does it run end-to-end 3 times without manual fixes? (Pass/Fail: `3/3`).
- **Error Handling:** Force a failure (e.g., missing folder). Does it alert you with a clear reason and a retry option? (Pass/Fail).

## 6. Task Capture + Personal Project Management

### Tool:
- Notion (consider: Todoist)

### Automation:
- Centralize tasks/docs, templates, quick capture, databases with views.

### Actionable Checklist:
- **Weekly Review View:** Create 3 views: (1) Today, (2) This Week, (3) Waiting On. Can you triage 20 tasks in under 10 minutes? (Pass/Fail).
- **Template Test:** Create a “Meeting Notes” template with decisions + next steps. Use it twice. Does it reduce note-taking friction? (Pass/Fail).

## 7. AI-Powered Focus + Website Blocking

### Tool:
- Freedom (consider: Cold Turkey)

### Automation:
- Block distracting sites/apps on schedules; protect deep-work blocks.

### Actionable Checklist:
- **Hard Block:** Schedule a 60-minute block and attempt to open 3 distracting sites. Are they blocked on every device you use for work? (Pass/Fail).
- **Recovery Plan:** Add an “allowed list” for true work sites. Can you do your job without constantly pausing the block? (Pass/Fail).

## 8. Document Search Across Your Stack

### Tool:
- Raycast (Mac) (consider: Alfred)

### Automation:
- Fast search + command palette for files, browser history, snippets, and app actions.

### Actionable Checklist:
- **10-Second Retrieval:** Pick 5 items you often hunt for (file, link, snippet). Can you open each in under 10 seconds using search + shortcuts? (Pass/Fail: `>=4/5`).
- **Snippet Library:** Save 10 reusable snippets (intro, FAQ, pricing reply). Do you use at least 3 within a week? (Pass/Fail).

## 9. New: “One Prompt, Many Outputs” Reusable Prompt Library

This is the fastest way to make AI tools feel like a workflow (not a toy): save 3–5 reusable prompts and run them every week.

### Tool:
- ChatGPT / Claude (consider: any LLM you already pay for)

### Automation:
- Standardize your thinking: meeting follow-ups, weekly plans, status updates, and decision memos.

### Actionable Checklist:
- **Create 3 Templates:** Save prompts for:
  - “Meeting follow-up email + action list”
  - “Weekly plan from tasks + calendar constraints”
  - “Decision memo: options, tradeoffs, recommendation”
  (Pass/Fail: all 3 exist and are reusable)
- **Run the Workflow:** Use those templates for one real week. Did you save at least 30 minutes total? (Pass/Fail: estimate `>=30 min`).

### Copy/Paste Prompt (Meeting Follow-Up)
Paste into your LLM after a meeting summary/transcript:

```
You are my executive assistant.

Context:
- Meeting summary: <paste>
- Attendees: <names>
- Deadline(s): <dates>

Output:
1) A 5-bullet recap (decisions + why)
2) Action items table: owner | task | due date | status
3) A short follow-up email I can send
Constraints: be specific, no vague tasks.
```

## 10. Lightweight Screen Recording for Async Updates

### Tool:
- Loom (consider: Screen Studio)

### Automation:
- Replace meetings with async walkthroughs (status, bug repros, feedback).

### Actionable Checklist:
- **Replace One Meeting:** Record a 3–5 minute Loom update instead of a sync. Does it eliminate at least one meeting this week? (Pass/Fail).
- **Clarity Test:** Ask one viewer: “What should I do next?” If they answer correctly without extra calls, keep it. (Pass/Fail).
